What happens in each phase?

The important part. Held in the growth window, the willemite crystals grow outward into branched starbursts. The hold temperature and how long you hold set the size and shape.

Why crash cool the kiln?

Once the kiln hits its peak temperature the glaze is fully melted and any crystals that were there get dissolved. If you cool it slowly from there, crystals start forming all over the place and stay small. Crashing the temperature down fast and then holding it steady gives you way more control — the crystals only really grow during that hold, so you end up with a few big starbursts instead of a bunch of tiny ones.

What's actually crystallizing?

The crystals are willemite, which forms when the zinc oxide and silica in the glaze combine in the melt. A little titanium dioxide gets added as a nucleating agent, basically giving the crystals a spot to start from. Once they get going they grow outward as branched, needle-like crystals radiating from the center — that's the starburst you see on the finished piece.
